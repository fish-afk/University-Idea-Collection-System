Terms and conditions

1. Introduction

The specific terms that are included to this agreement (and form part of this agreement) and the personalized offer letters that provide additional details about employment, such as job title, grade, rate of pay, hours worked, and the date of commencement of duties including continuous employment date, should be read in addition to these terms and conditions of employment, which apply to all staff members.

2. Job description

Each employee should have a job description, which will be given to them when they start working for the university. Job descriptions will only be taken into consideration as a guide to the accountabilities associated with the role, not as exact or comprehensive specifications of duties and responsibilities. Staff members must also complete any additional or alternative tasks that the university deems necessary from time to time in order to properly perform their duties. They must also take any training that the university deems necessary.

3. Permanent employment

3.1 Offer letters will make reference to any prior employment with the University or a previous employer, such as NHS Trusts, that is to be counted toward the period of continuous service.

3.2 The university will take into account prior service for the purpose of calculating maternity benefits for staff members transferring directly from another university within the European Economic Area. The period of continuous employment for statutory purposes will begin on their start date with the University of Bristol for all other contractual and legal purposes.

4. Salary

4.1 Basic salary accrues over time and is paid monthly by credit transfer into a staff member's designated bank or building society account, typically on the 26th day of each calendar month, after deductions for tax, national insurance, and any other agreed-upon or legally required deductions, including, as necessary, deductions for pension contributions.

4.2 Annual reviews of the University's pay structure are conducted, and any revisions typically take effect on August 1 of each year.

4.3 Associate Professors and Grade M employees are the only exceptions. All other staff, with the exception of those two groups, will advance yearly on the pertinent incremental date to the highest service increment point applicable to their grade, barring circumstances in which subpar performance has already been proven through the application of the Capability Procedure.

5. Deduction

According to the Employment Rights Act of 1996, the University is permitted to deduct from a staff member's salary any amounts owed, including overpayments for loans or advances made to them by the University and overpayments for annual leave taken in excess of their entitlement. Without first informing the person, no deductions will be made.

6. work environment

The University reserves the right to require staff to work temporarily or permanently at any other place of work or location that the University may reasonably require. A member of staff's regular place of employment will be such sites of the University and associated NHS Trusts as are requested. A staff member may be required to travel both domestically and abroad as part of their duties.

7. sickness

7.1 Staff must call the university as soon as possible (typically by 10 a.m.) on the first working day of their absence to notify it that they won't be able to report to work due to illness or another incapacity. Staff members must specify their absence's cause, their anticipated return date, and/or the time they will next contact you with more details.

A staff member who has been absent due to illness is required to submit a self-certification form to the university upon their return. If the absence lasts for eight days or longer in a row (including weekends), the student must submit a fit note to the university, which must be issued by a specific healthcare professional. To cover the entire absence, additional fit notes will be needed. The staff member is accountable for updating the University on their whereabouts and anticipated return date.

7.2 From the first day of a sickness absence, the University will typically pay sick pay in accordance with the following rules.

Length of Service Full Pay
First 3 months of service 2 weeks
Remaining 9 months of first year of service 9 weeks
Second and third year of service 13 weeks

After fifth year of service 26 weeks


7.3 The normal rate of pay for a staff member is considered university sick pay. Any entitlement to Statutory Sick Pay (SSP) must be paid in addition to half pay but included in full pay.

7.4 The normal rate of pay for a staff member is considered university sick pay. Any entitlement to Statutory Sick Pay (SSP) must be paid in addition to half pay but included in full pay.

7.5 The normal rate of pay for a staff member is considered university sick pay. Any entitlement to Statutory Sick Pay (SSP) must be paid in addition to half pay but included in full pay.

7.6 The period of service used to determine sick pay eligibility is the amount of time that was worked continuously from the date of hire to the first day of absence. The effective period of service for determining the allowance due will start on the date of re-appointment if a break in continuous service results from resignation and re-employment. 7.7 The length of service as of the first day of absence is used to determine eligibility for sick pay. For any days of paid sick leave taken in the 12 months prior to the first day of their current absence, deductions will be made.

8. expense

Subject to their compliance with any policy or procedure the University may issue from time to time with regard to expenses and their production of any necessary evidence of expenses, the University shall reimburse staff for all expenses wholly and necessarily incurred by them in the proper performance of their duties.

Staff must obtain full medical insurance if they travel abroad as part of their duties. Before departing, staff members should make arrangements for insurance with the university's insurance officer. Only insurance obtained through the University Scheme is eligible for reimbursement.

9. Copyright and Design

All copyright works including computer software and all designs whether registered or unregistered created by staff members alone or in collaboration with others during the course of employment must be promptly reported to the university. The rights to such works or designs shall typically be the property of the University in accordance with the provisions of the Copyright, Designs and Patents Act 1988 and the Registered Designs Act 1949, as amended. Staff members are required to consult with the University so that the proper arrangements can be made in the case of the commercial exploitation of copyright works (including computer software) and designs (whether registered or unregistered) made in the course of employment.

Staff members must notify the Research and Enterprise Development Office and University Secretary of any potential patents, copyright claims, and designs through their head of department. Staff members must speak with the Registrar and the Research and Enterprise Development Office through their head of department before signing any contracts with external organizations on the university's behalf.

10. Attire and tools for the job

10.1 If staff are issued with workwear or equipment they:

A. must ensure that they use it whenever necessary, or as required, and in accordance with the University's Health and Safety Policy.

B. must tell the University as soon as it needs replacing whether due to damage, defect or for any other reasons.

11. Communication Interception

Under the terms of the Regulation of Investigatory Powers Act of 2000, the General Data Protection Regulation [Data Protection Act 2018], the Telecommunications [Lawful Business Practice] [Interception of Communications] Regulations of 2000, and any other applicable laws in effect at the time, the University may, if it considers it reasonable in all the circumstances, monitor and record a member of staff's communications [including but not limited to emails, instant messages, and telephone calls].
